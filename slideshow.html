Experiment Overview

The goal of this experiment is to investigate how people deconstruct and make sense of temporal data.

We will show you some data that comes from a simulated learning environment in New York where school students have worked to control the flows of water in a closed system over time. Below you see a representation of the water flows in the system and a line graph that shows the level of water in the four biomes (areas) of the simulation.

Your task is to identify points in time where the flows of water in the simulation change significantly.

2 /

Description of Connected Worlds

Connected Worlds is a simulated environment where the students control water flows and plant trees that allow animals to flourish. It consists of water sources (the Waterfall, the Mountain Valley and the Reservoir) and four biomes (the Desert, the Plains, the Jungle and the Wetlands) where the trees grow and the animals find their habitat. The students interact on the floor and move the red logs to redirect water to the four biomes such that life can be sustained.

3 /

Description of Water Level Graph

The line graph shows the level of water in the four biomes. When the students make a decision to re-route water in the simulation, we see the corresponding water in a biome rise if the biome was targeted, or drop if the water was directed away from the biome.

The image below has a red line marked where the water in the Jungle changes from a rising trend to a trend that is decreasing. The video can be consulted to confirm that the students redirected one of the logs that caused this dramatic change in the water level.

Description of User Interface

You will see an interface that looks like the following image:

This interface presents a session from students interacting with Connected Worlds. Your task is to click the play button and watch the black time marker line and the video as the session plays and to notice when you think a major change in the water flows occurs. You can use the play/pause button, the jump controls or the left and right arrows to navigate through the session.

When you think a large change occurs, then pause the video and use the left/right arrow buttons to position the black cursor over the time that the change occured. Click the "Mark as major change in water flows" button to tag this point in time (you can also use the Spacebar to mark that as a change point).

Remember to click the "Done Labelling" button when you are finished.
