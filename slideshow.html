Experiment Overview

The goal of this experiment is to investigate how people deconstruct and make sense of temporal data.

We will show you some data that comes from a simulated learning environment in New York where high school students have worked to control the flows of water in a closed system over time. Below you see a representation of the water flows in the system and a line graph that shows the level of water in the four biomes (areas) of the simulation.

Your task is to identify points in time where the dynamics in the simulation change significantly.

Description of Connected Worlds

Connected Worlds is the simulation environment that the students interact in. It consists of a water sources (the Waterfall, the Mountain Valley and the Reservoir) and four biomes (the Desert, the Plains, the Jungle and the Wetlands) where trees grow and animals flourish. The studnets interact on the flood plain and move the red logs on the floor to redirect water to the four biomes such that life can be sustained.

Description of Time Series

The line graph shows the level of water in the four biomes. When the students make a decision to re-route water in the simulation, we see the corresponding water in a biome rise (if the biome was targetted) or drop (if the water was directed away from the biome).

The image below has a red line marked where the water in the Jungle changes from a rising trend to a trend that is decreasing. The video can be consulted to confirm that the students redirected one of the logs that caused this dramatic change in the water level.

Description of User Interface

You will see an interface that looks like the image below:

This interface presents one 8 minute session of the students interacting with the simulation (don't worry, the video is much faster than 8 minutes as it has been sped up). Your task is to click the play button and watch the black line and the video as the session plays and to notice when you think a major change in the water flows occures. You can use the pause button, the jump controls or the left and right arrows to navigate through the session.

When you think a large change occurs, then pause the video and use the left/right arrow buttons to position the black cursor over the time that the change occured. Click the "Mark this as a Change Point" button to tag this point in time.
